What is Arbitrum?

Optimistic Rollups that scale Ethereum infinitely.

Arbitrum bundles thousands of transactions together, executes them off-chain at lightning speed, and posts a single cryptographic proof back to Ethereum.

  • Layer 2: Built on top of Ethereum
  • Optimistic Rollups: Assumes validity, verified by fraud proofs
  • Transaction Batching: Massive data compression
  • Ethereum Settlement: Inherits L1 security

How It Works

The anatomy of an Arbitrum transaction.

1

Submit

User submits transaction to Arbitrum RPC.

2

Execute

Arbitrum sequencer executes transaction instantly with low fee.

3

Batch

Transactions are compressed and grouped into rollups.

4

Submit to L1

Rollups are posted to Ethereum mainnet as calldata.

5

Finalize

Ethereum secures the state change cryptographically.

Ethereum's architecture prioritizes decentralization and security over speed. This leads to congestion and high fees during peak usage. Layer 2 networks like Arbitrum solve this by processing transactions off-chain while relying on Ethereum for underlying security.
